# Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI fraud is a common type of cybercrime in which attackers trick users into sending money or sharing sensitive banking details. It usually happens through fake calls, messages, or payment requests.

A typical scenario involves a scammer contacting the victim by pretending to be a bank official, customer support agent, or buyer. First, they build trust by speaking confidently and using urgent language. Next, they ask the victim to click on a link, scan a QR code, or approve a payment request. Many victims mistakenly believe that scanning a QR code will help them receive money, but instead, it results in money being deducted from their account. In some cases, scammers also ask for OTPs or PINs to gain full access to the account.

Students, elderly people, and individuals who are not fully aware of digital payment systems are the most common targets. The consequences include financial loss, stress, and sometimes difficulty in recovering the stolen money. Victims may also lose trust in online payment systems.

This case highlights the importance of digital awareness and careful verification before making any online transactions.
